Introduction & Learning Philosophy

I am Yash Talele, a Chemical Engineering undergraduate at Pandit Deendayal Energy University, Gandhinagar. My academic journey has been shaped by a deep curiosity about how scientific principles translate into real-world impact — from industrial processes to sustainability initiatives.

My learning philosophy rests on three pillars:

  • Interdisciplinary Thinking — I believe the most meaningful innovations emerge at the crossroads of multiple disciplines. My work spans process engineering, data analysis, sustainability research, and management.
  • Learning by Doing — Whether it's optimizing a chemical plant's operations during an internship or leading a 60-member student chapter, I prioritize hands-on experience over passive consumption of knowledge.
  • Purpose-Driven Engineering — Every skill I develop and every project I undertake is guided by the question: "How does this contribute to a more sustainable and equitable world?"

These values have guided me from my early education in Jalgaon, Maharashtra, through competitive examinations, to my current role as a research-oriented engineering student committed to continuous growth.

Interdisciplinary Projects & Research

Project 01

Carbon Capture Efficiency using Ionic Liquids & DES Blends

Objective

To evaluate and optimize CO₂ capture efficiency using ionic liquids and deep eutectic solvent (DES) blends through NMR-based molecular interaction studies.

Methodology
  • NMR spectroscopy to analyze molecular interactions and solubility parameters
  • Comparative analysis of ionic liquid-DES blends vs. conventional amine solvents
  • Energy efficiency assessment and thermodynamic modeling
Outcomes & Innovations
  • Identified promising IL-DES combinations with superior CO₂ absorption capacity
  • Demonstrated lower regeneration energy requirements compared to MEA baselines
  • Results contribute to greener carbon capture technologies
Challenges & Solutions

Interpreting complex NMR spectra required iterative refinement of experimental conditions and close collaboration with faculty advisors and analytical lab teams.
